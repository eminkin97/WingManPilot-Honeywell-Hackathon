Team Wingman

Wingman vision in action

Example 1:

Pilot - "Wingman?"

Wingman - "Roger!"

Pilot - "Give me information about my next flight"

Wingman - "Your next flight is (date/time) and goes from LaGuardia to LAX with an estimated flight time of 5 hours and 23 minutes. Your commute distance to LaGuardia will take (estimated time). Your wake up call is set for (date/time). Your Uber driver will pick you up at (date/time/destination)." [A calendar and map display on the screen with current location, takeoff point and landing point.]

Pilot - "Are there any weather disturbances between destinations?"

Wingman - "Affirmative. You'll have headwinds that range from 5 mph to 30 mph which could cause some turbulence over Pittsburg. Humidity is at [measured unit]. There is potential for icing as your approach Denver, Colorado." [A map display shows animated weather pattern over the flight path]

Pilot - "How can I reduce fuel consumption and boost efficiency with this aircraft?"

Wingman - "Pre-flight, you can [instructions]. At take off, you can [instructions]. At cruising altitude be sure to [instructions]. Upon landing, you can [instructions]." [A flight path diagram shows the plane at the different stages of the trip and instructions at each stage.]

Example 2:

Maintenance Manager - "Wingman?"

Wingman - "Roger!"

Maintenance - "Are the any parts that need urgent attention?"

Wingman - "Affirmative. This aircraft needs 3 part that need urgent attention. The are 5 parts that are approaching urgent maintenance within 1 week." [A diagram of the plane appears displaying parts in various states of criticality]


Wingman - "Cabin oxygen sensors need to be replaced at the rear of the cabin.

[A display shows exactly where the sensors are located along with pertinent info.]


Maintenance - "Please show me the wing vibration records of the last flight."

Wingman - "Wing vibrations on the last flight were within normal operating parameters." [Displays a wing vibration chart from takeoff to landing.]


Example 3:

Stewardess - "Wingman?"

Wingman - "Roger!"

Stewardess - "Are there and special needs passengers boarding this flight?"

Wingman - "Affirmative. There are 2. First, we have a passenger with sight impairment and is accompanied by a service animal. Second, we have senior citizen that requires assistance boarding." [A seating chart displays with their current seat assignment].

Stewardess - "Please contact the ticketing agents and have them move them to the bulkhead section."

Wingman - "Roger that! I will update you on their seating arrangement."
